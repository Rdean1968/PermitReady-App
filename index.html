The process

From signed agreement to paid project

Every step is designed to protect you, the homeowner, and the integrity of the project.

1
Homeowner signs a PermitReady platform agreement
Before their project is published, every homeowner signs an agreement to work through PermitReady. They are not browsing — they are committed.
Free for homeowners
2
Project brief is reviewed and published
Every brief is personally reviewed before going live. Real budget, real scope, real homeowner. No junk projects.
Quality controlled
3
Pro pays $500 deposit to claim the project
Once you review the full brief and decide to claim it, a $500 deposit is collected and held in PermitReady escrow. This deposit is fully applied toward your final fee — it is not an additional cost.
$500 deposit → escrow Applied to your fee
4
All communication goes through PermitReady
Until PermitReady's fee is collected, all emails and messages between you and the homeowner are routed through our platform. This protects both parties and ensures full transparency.
Protected messaging
5
You agree on a price — homeowner's first payment goes through escrow
When you and the homeowner agree on a contract price, the homeowner's first project payment flows through PermitReady's escrow account. PermitReady deducts the 1.5% fee (minus the $500 deposit already paid) and releases the remainder directly to you.
Escrow → fee deducted → GC paid
6
PermitReady steps away — you own the relationship
Once our fee is collected and funds are released, PermitReady has zero further involvement or liability. You and the homeowner communicate directly for the remainder of the project. It is yours entirely.
PermitReady done · You take over

The fee structure

Exactly how the money works

No surprises. No hidden fees. Here is every dollar explained.

1
You pay $500 to claim the project
Collected upfront when you claim a project. Goes directly into PermitReady escrow — not spent, held.
$500 deposit → escrow
2
Homeowner's first payment flows through PermitReady
When the contract is signed and the homeowner makes their first payment to start the project, that payment is routed through PermitReady's escrow account.
Homeowner's deposit → PermitReady escrow
3
PermitReady deducts the 1.5% fee
From the homeowner's payment, PermitReady deducts 1.5% of the total agreed contract value. Your $500 deposit is credited against this amount.
1.5% fee − $500 credit = balance due
4
You receive the remainder immediately
After the fee is deducted, the full remainder of the homeowner's payment is released directly to you. PermitReady's involvement ends here entirely.
Remainder → your account · PermitReady done
Example — $250,000 project
Contract value$250,000
PermitReady fee (1.5%)$3,750
Less deposit already paid− $500
Balance deducted from escrow$3,250
You receive$246,250
PermitReady's total take: $3,750 (1.5%). Your total cost: $3,250 after deposit credit. After this, the project is entirely yours.

Any contractor found to have bypassed PermitReady and contacted a homeowner directly — before our fee is collected — will be subject to a platform integrity fee. This is not a lead service fee. Common questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about working with PermitReady.

Is PermitReady really free for homeowners? +
Yes, 100% free. Homeowners never pay PermitReady anything — not now, not ever. Our fee comes from the professional who wins your project, and only after you've signed a contract with them. You get matched with vetted pros at no cost.
How do you vet the contractors and pros? +
Every pro on PermitReady is manually reviewed. We verify active Washington State licenses, check insurance coverage, review past project history, and confirm they're in good standing. We only approve established professionals with a track record in King and Snohomish County.
What if I don't like the pro you match me with? +
No obligation whatsoever. If the match isn't right, just let us know. You're never locked in, and we can connect you with a different professional. Our goal is finding you the right fit, not just any fit.
Why should contractors pay 1.5% when they can find their own leads? +
These aren't leads — they're signed clients. Every homeowner has already agreed to work with the pro who claims them. No competing bids, no tire-kickers, no chasing. You pay only on projects you actually win. Most contractors spend 5-10% of revenue on marketing for uncertain results. This is 1.5% for a done deal.
What makes this different from Thumbtack or Angi? +
On those platforms, you're one of 5+ contractors bidding on the same job — and you pay just to send a quote. On PermitReady, when you claim a project, it's yours exclusively. No competition. The homeowner has already signed an agreement to work with you. You pay nothing until the project closes.
What if the project falls through after I claim it? +
Your $500 deposit is fully refundable if the deal doesn't close — minus payment processing fees, which we credit back to your account for your next claim. You only pay the 1.5% success fee when money actually changes hands and the project moves forward.
